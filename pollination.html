Process of Pollination

Pollen grains, produced in the anther, are transferred to the stigma (the receptive surface of the pistil). This transfer can occur through various mechanisms, such as wind, water, or animal vectors like insects.

Once on the stigma, the pollen grain is received and typically held there, initiating the next stage.

The pollen grain germinates, meaning it starts to grow a pollen tube down through the style (the stalk connecting the stigma to the ovary).

The pollen tube delivers sperm cells to the ovule within the ovary. These sperm cells then fuse with egg cells in the ovule, resulting in fertilization.
