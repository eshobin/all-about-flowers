All About Flowers!

The heads of young sunflowers rotate to follow the sun's path across the sky, a behavior called heliotropism.

Some plants, like the Venus flytrap and pitcher plant, are carnivorous, trapping and digesting insects for nutrients.

Flowers have been used for centuries to communicate messages, a practice known as floriography.

Some flowers have specialized structures that can vibrate in response to the sounds of bees, according to a study by scientists.
